Cartoon — a stylized, expressive, Western animation-inspired art style characterized by exaggerated proportions, bold outlines, squash-and-stretch deformation, rubber-hose or solid-drawing construction, and a playful, energetic visual language rooted in Western theatrical animation traditions.

LINE WORK:
- Bold, confident, expressive outlines — primary silhouette lines are thick (3–5px equivalent) and taper naturally with applied pressure.
- Line weight variation is expressive and intentional: lines thicken on the downward, shadow-side of forms and thin on the upward, light-catching side.
- Internal lines for features and clothing details are medium-weight, distinct from the primary outline.
- Linework has energy and dynamism — slight curves and flowing arcs give even static poses a sense of movement and personality.
- Ink lines may show slight brush character — not as loose as hand-drawn, but not mechanically sterile either.

SHADING AND RENDERING:
- Simplified cel shading with 2–3 flat tone levels per color (base, shadow, optional secondary shadow and/or highlight).
- Shadow bands are soft-hard — edges may be slightly softened or have a subtle blurred quality, giving a warmer feel than strict hard-cel anime shading.
- Shadows are rounded and follow the form stylistically — they celebrate shape rather than simulate physics.
- Specular highlights on shiny surfaces (eyes, buttons, metals) are bright, clean, round dot-shapes or sharp wedge shapes.
- Eye highlights: large, clean, expressive white reflections within bright, saturated iris colors.

COLOR PALETTE:
- Highly saturated, warm, inviting colors — the palette should feel friendly, accessible, and immediately appealing.
- Avoid overly dark or desaturated tones in primary character regions; shadows are achieved through mid-dark shades of the base hue rather than neutral greys.
- Color choices are emotionally and personality-driven: heroes often wear warm, bright hues; villains may use cooler or more saturated contrast colors.
- Background environments use bright, storybook-like colors that establish mood without competing with characters.

TEXTURE TREATMENT:
- Character surfaces are mostly smooth with minimal texture. Clothing and skin have clean, flat fills.
- Subtle paper or painted texture may appear across the image as a light stylistic grain, but should not overpower the flat cel aesthetic.
- Environmental elements (trees, rocks, water) may use simplified, stylized texture patterns — repeated, decorative, and clearly non-photographic.

CHARACTER PROPORTIONS:
- Exaggerated, expressive proportions: large heads (1:3 to 1:5 head-to-body ratio), oversized eyes, small stylized noses and mouths, and simplified ear and hand shapes.
- Body shapes are built from clear, readable silhouettes using simple geometric primitives: circles, ovals, rectangles, and organic blobs.
- Squash-and-stretch deformation is embraced — characters can compress and elongate for comedic or dramatic effect.
- Facial expressions are broad and readable from a distance: large mouth shapes, exaggerated eye widening or narrowing, dramatic brow arching.

KEY VISUAL CHARACTERISTICS:
- Bold, clean, expressive outlines with intentional weight variation.
- Bright, saturated, personality-driven color palette.
- Simplified but character-rich forms with exaggerated proportions.
- Expressive, emotionally amplified poses and facial expressions.
- The overall aesthetic should evoke classic and modern Western theatrical animation — productions such as Disney, DreamWorks, Cartoon Network, Nickelodeon, or modern titles like Cuphead, Hilda, or The Owl House.
