Hyper-Realistic — the highest tier of visual fidelity, targeting photographic realism with the quality of professional studio photography, cinematic VFX, or next-generation real-time rendering. Every element must be rendered with the accuracy, physical correctness, and fine detail of a high-resolution photograph taken under controlled, professional lighting conditions.

LINE WORK:
- There are no visible outlines. Form is defined entirely through value, color, and physically accurate edge behavior — exactly as in a photograph.
- Edge sharpness is controlled by depth of field: surfaces in the focal plane have tack-sharp, high-frequency edge detail; surfaces outside the depth of field have progressively softer, bokeh-influenced edges.
- No artistic edge treatment of any kind. Boundaries between surfaces follow strict physical law.

SHADING AND RENDERING:
- Full physically-based rendering (PBR) approach: every surface must respond to light according to its real-world material properties (metallic/dielectric distinction, roughness, IOR — Index of Refraction).
- Lighting is cinematic and precise: one or more defined light sources with physically accurate falloff, color temperature (expressed in Kelvin), hardness/softness, and cast shadows with correct penumbra.
- Subsurface scattering is mandatory for all skin surfaces — light visibly penetrates and scatters through the dermis, producing the characteristic warm, translucent glow of real human skin near light sources.
- Eyes must have fully realized physical rendering: corneal specular reflection of the light source and environment, iris depth and texture, pupil dilation consistent with lighting conditions, subtle moisture sheen on the eyeball surface.
- Hair is rendered at the individual strand level in focal areas — thousands of individual filaments with correct specular dual-lobe shading (Marschner or similar), capturing the unique way hair reflects and transmits light.
- Contact shadows and micro-shadows in skin pores, fabric weaves, and surface micro-geometry are visible and physically accurate.
- Global illumination effects are present: indirect bounce light from surrounding surfaces visibly affects shadow areas.

COLOR AND TONE:
- Colors match the physical reality of the represented materials under the given lighting conditions.
- White balance is set to a specific, realistic color temperature — surfaces under warm incandescent lighting have a warm cast; outdoor daylight subjects have cooler, bluer ambient light in shadows.
- Post-processing tone mapping is applied to manage dynamic range: bright highlights bloom subtly; deep shadows retain some shadow detail (as in modern HDR photography), unless a specific cinematic look dictates otherwise.
- No stylistic color grading or saturation boosting — the color profile should appear achievable by a real-world camera with professional color calibration.

TEXTURE TREATMENT:
- All surfaces are rendered with photographic-quality texture maps at the highest possible level of detail.
- Skin: visible pores, fine surface hair, subtle blemishes, capillary redness at thin-skin areas (ears, nose, eyelids), realistic makeup or weathering as appropriate.
- Fabric: individual thread visibility at close range, correct fiber sheen and directionality, wear and micro-fraying at stress points.
- Metal: precise environment reflection with material-accurate reflectivity (e.g., polished steel vs. brushed aluminum vs. oxidized copper are visually distinct), surface micro-scratches, and oil fingerprints where contextually appropriate.
- Stone, wood, organic materials: fully detailed surface micro-geometry visible as normal-map-level surface variation.

DEPTH OF FIELD AND OPTICS:
- Apply realistic depth of field consistent with a specific simulated lens (e.g., 85mm f/1.8 for portraiture, 24mm f/8 for environmental wide shots).
- Background elements exhibit smooth, creamy bokeh blur consistent with the simulated aperture.
- Lens effects (very subtle chromatic aberration, vignetting, minimal diffraction on point light sources) may be applied as a light post-processing step to reinforce photographic authenticity.

CHARACTER ACCURACY:
- Human anatomy must be perfect — proportion, skeletal structure, muscle definition, and fat distribution must be exactly as they would appear on a real human body under the given lighting conditions.
- Facial features have every detail of a real human face: eyelashes as individual strands, eyebrow hairs as individual filaments, subtle skin micro-texture and pore structure visible in the focal region.
- Clothing behaves as real fabric: drape, compression, stretch, and shadow are physically correct for the specific cloth type.

KEY VISUAL CHARACTERISTICS:
- Photographic-grade fidelity indistinguishable from professional studio or cinematic photography.
- Physically-based material responses: PBR metallic, specular, and diffuse behavior.
- Strand-level hair rendering with dual-lobe specular shading.
- Subsurface scattering on all organic surfaces.
- Depth of field consistent with a simulated real-world camera lens.
- The final image should be indistinguishable from a photograph or frame from a AAA cinematic cutscene rendered by a studio such as Blur Studio, Weta FX, or ILM.
