Minimalist — a clean, reductive design-first art style that distills every subject to its most essential, recognizable form using the fewest possible visual elements, maximum negative space, and strictly controlled geometry.

LINE WORK:
- Lines are clean, precise, and uniform in weight — mechanically perfect rather than organic.
- Outlines are either completely absent (pure silhouette / flat-fill approach) or used sparingly as single, consistent-weight strokes.
- No hatching, cross-hatching, or decorative linework. Every line must serve a structural purpose.
- Geometric simplification: complex organic shapes (hair, foliage, clouds) are reduced to smooth curves, simple polygons, or clean arcs.
- Details are omitted aggressively — if a detail doesn't contribute to recognition or composition, it is removed.

SHADING AND RENDERING:
- Shading is minimal to nonexistent. Prefer flat, solid fills over any gradient or tonal rendering.
- If depth is needed, use at most 2–3 flat tones per object (base + one shadow + one highlight), separated by hard, clean edges. No gradual tonal transitions.
- Shadows may be represented as a single, simplified flat shape in a darker tone of the same hue — not a soft drop shadow.
- Specular highlights are either absent or represented as a single simple geometric shape (circle, teardrop) in white or near-white.

COLOR PALETTE:
- Strictly limited palette — typically 3 to 8 total colors in the entire image, including neutrals.
- Colors are bold, purposeful, and carefully harmonized. Each color is assigned a clear role: primary, accent, neutral, or background.
- High contrast between figure and background is mandatory. Negative space is treated as an active design element.
- Monochromatic, duotone, or tightly analogous palettes are common and preferred. Avoid complex or busy multi-color schemes.
- Background is typically a single flat color or clean white/near-white, never a complex environment.

TEXTURE TREATMENT:
- Surfaces are completely texture-free. No grain, noise, canvas texture, brushstrokes, or surface variation of any kind.
- Every fill region is a single, perfectly uniform, flat color value — no variation within a fill.
- Material differentiation is suggested through color choice, not texture.

COMPOSITION:
- Generous negative space is not a problem to be filled — it is the primary compositional tool.
- Subjects are reduced to iconic, instantly readable silhouettes.
- Alignment and spacing follow strict geometric principles — grid-based layouts, centered focal points, or deliberate asymmetric balance.
- No environmental clutter; backgrounds carry only what is strictly compositionally necessary.

KEY VISUAL CHARACTERISTICS:
- Extreme simplicity and clarity — the image communicates instantly at a glance.
- Perfect geometric precision — clean curves, sharp corners, flawless alignment.
- Every single element is intentional; nothing is accidental or decorative.
- The overall aesthetic should evoke modern flat design, Swiss graphic design, app iconography, or clean vector illustration for tech and branding contexts.
